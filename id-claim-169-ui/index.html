About

What Claim 169 is — and why it matters

01
The concept

What is Claim 169?

Claim 169 defines a standardized, CBOR-based QR code format for securely embedding identity data in a machine-readable optical format.

It enables:

  • Storage of identity data + photo within a QR code
  • Digitally signed credentials issued by trusted authorities
  • Offline verification without requiring connectivity
  • Facial authentication using embedded image + data
  • Compact encoding using CBOR, CWT, and COSE for QR efficiency

The design uses:

CBORRFC 7049compact data representation
CWTRFC 8392token structure
COSERFC 8152signing and encryption
ECC / Ed25519smaller, efficient signatures

This allows identity credentials to be portable, verifiable, and interoperable across systems.

Cryptographically Signed Offline-First Platform Agnostic Open Working Group

A secure, compact, and interoperable way to carry identity — in a single QR code.

02
Why it matters

Why Claim 169?

In low-connectivity and cross-border environments, traditional identity systems often fail — leaving millions without reliable access to trusted identity.

People may:

  • Lose physical credentials
  • Lack access to smartphones
  • Depend on unreliable or unavailable networks
  • Struggle with expensive smart or plastic cards
  • Forget passwords or PINs required for verification
  • Use weak or easily guessable PINs (e.g. 0000, 1234)
  • Face challenges when credentials are damaged or compromised
  • Experience identity data exposure during verification

Making secure and reliable verification difficult when it matters most.

Bridging the identity gap

Claim 169 solves this by enabling identity to be carried in a single QR code — securely and portably.

  • Digitally signed identity data + photo
  • Usable on devices or as a printed credential
  • Verifiable without internet connectivity
  • Reduces dependency on passwords and/or smartcards
  • Enables trusted offline identity verification across systems and borders
  • Minimizes unnecessary data exposure

Even without a phone, individuals can present a printed QR code and securely access services with confidence.

By supporting both digital and non-digital use cases, Claim 169 brings together:

Accessibility Portability Privacy Trust

Enabling secure, high-assurance identity verification — anywhere, anytime.

Process

From Identity to Trusted Verification

  1. 01

    Generate QR Code

    Secure identity data is encoded and signed into a QR

  2. 02

    Issue Credential

    QR is shared digitally or printed for the individual

  3. 03

    Present QR Code

    User presents QR at verification point

  4. 04

    Scan & Decode

    Verifier scans and extracts identity data

  5. 05

    Verify Identity

    Signature validated + face match confirms presence

  6. 06

    Access Service

    Identity verified → service delivered

Claim 169 defines 75+ standardised identity attributes across CWT headers, demographic fields (claims 1–23), and biometric fields (claims 50–65). Each QR code encodes a cryptographically-signed CBOR structure that verifiers can authenticate without any network dependency.

Use Cases

In Practice: Ecosystem Evolution & Adoption

Live deployments demonstrating trusted offline identity verification across national and sectoral contexts.

Ecosystem Evolution

Philippines — PhilSys & Financial Inclusion

PhilSys, the Philippines' national digital ID system built on MOSIP, enabled millions of previously unbanked residents to open bank accounts for the first time, significantly advancing financial inclusion. As adoption expanded into low-connectivity and assisted-service environments, the need for trusted offline identity verification became increasingly evident, helping shape the evolution of the Claim 169 QR code specification for secure, portable, offline-verifiable credentials.

Financial Inclusion Offline Verification Portable Credentials Digital Identity

Country Implementation

Ethiopia — Fayda Digital ID

Ethiopia's national ID system enables offline identity verification using QR-based credentials and biometrics. Supported by TECH5, it powers secure authentication and service access across public and private sectors.

Uganda — Digital Identity Ecosystem

Uganda is modernizing its identity systems using MOSIP-based approaches to enable secure, interoperable identity verification across services, with growing support for offline and portable credentials using Claim 169.

Where Claim 169 can be Used

Potential use cases enabling trusted offline identity verification across humanitarian, government, and development contexts.

  • Humanitarian

    Refugee & Displaced Person ID

    UNHCR and partner agencies use Claim 169 QR codes on physical cards for displaced populations who lack internet access. A single scan verifies identity, biometrics, and entitlements offline.

    UNHCR PartnerOffline

  • Government

    National ID Cards

    National ID programs embed Claim 169 QR codes on physical cards, allowing authorities to verify identity during power outages, in rural areas, or at land border crossings without network access.

    Physical CardRural AccessBorder Control

  • Healthcare

    Proof of Vaccination

    Individuals can present QR codes to verify vaccination status at checkpoints, travel hubs, or healthcare facilities — even without connectivity. Digitally signed data ensures authenticity, while optional biometric verification confirms the right individual receives care.

    No Connectivity

  • Cross-border

    International Travel

    Travelers crossing borders in regions with limited digital infrastructure can be verified by scanning the Claim 169 QR code. Works even when central databases are unreachable.

    Border ControlOffline

  • Sectoral ID

    Sectoral Identity Credentials

    Claim 169 enables issuance of trusted, sector-specific IDs that can be verified across services — ensuring secure, interoperable access even without connectivity.

    InteroperableTrusted Identity

  • Financial Inclusion

    Unbanked Population Onboarding

    Financial service providers can onboard unbanked individuals using Claim 169 credentials, enabling KYC verification without requiring document uploads or real-time API calls.

    KYCFinancial Access

  • And More…

    Claim 169 is adaptable to any context requiring secure, offline-capable identity verification.
